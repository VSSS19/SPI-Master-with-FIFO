Excited to share that our team secured 3rd Prize at the VLSI Make-A-Thon 2026 organized by the Centre for Nanoelectronics and VLSI Design (CNVD), VIT Chennai.
🔹 Problem Statement: SPI Master with FIFO (ASIC Implementation)
 🔹 Domain: Digital IC Design
🔬 What We Engineered
We architected and implemented a configurable SPI Master IP featuring:
• CPOL/CPHA programmability
 • 8/16-bit transfer capability
 • Integrated 16-depth FIFO with interrupt generation
 • Baud-rate clock divider derived from a 200 MHz system clock
 • Timing closure targeted at 200 MHz
🏗 Implementation Depth
This was executed through the complete RTL-to-GDSII flow:
• RTL design using Verilog
 • Functional verification via structured testbench
 • Logic synthesis and physical implementation
 • Floorplanning and power planning (rings & stripes)
 • Clock Tree Synthesis (CTS)
 • Routing and post-CTS timing optimization
 • Achieved clean timing convergence at 200 MHz
This experience strengthened my understanding of:
✔ Backend-aware RTL design
 ✔ Timing closure at high frequency
 ✔ PPA (Power, Performance, Area) trade-offs
 ✔ ASIC implementation constraints
